# Recognizing Impairment

## Signs of Impairment

Know the signs in yourself and coworkers:

### Physical Signs
- Slurred speech
- Unsteady balance or stumbling
- Bloodshot or glassy eyes
- Lack of coordination
- Excessive sweating or shaking

### Behavioral Signs
- Unusual aggression or hostility
- Excessive giggling or mood swings
- Paranoia or anxiety
- Difficulty concentrating
- Delayed reactions

### Performance Signs
- Missed turns or exits
- Erratic driving
- Forgetting procedures
- Poor judgment decisions
- Increased accidents or near-misses

## If You Suspect Impairment

### For Yourself
- **Do not drive**
- Contact your supervisor immediately
- Be honest about the situation

### For a Coworker
- Do not confront aggressively
- Report concerns to supervisor
- Document specific observations
- Do not let impaired person drive

## Your Responsibility

Everyone is responsible for safety:
- Report impairment concerns
- Support coworkers getting help
- Never allow impaired driving
- Protect yourself and others

## Confidentiality

Reports are handled confidentially:
- No retaliation for good-faith reports
- Focus is on safety, not punishment
- Support available for those seeking help
