# Managing Distractions

## Common Driving Distractions

### Mobile Phones
- **Risk**: 4x more likely to crash when using phone
- **Solution**: Use hands-free only when necessary
- **Best practice**: Pull over for important calls

### Eating and Drinking
- Takes hands off the wheel
- Spills can cause sudden reactions
- **Solution**: Eat before or after driving

### Passengers
- Conversations divert attention
- Children can be particularly distracting
- **Solution**: Set expectations before departure

### Navigation Systems
- Programming while driving is dangerous
- Looking at maps takes eyes off road
- **Solution**: Program route before starting

## The 2-Second Glance Rule

A 2-second glance at 60 km/h means **33 meters of blind travel**.

- Keep eyes on the road as much as possible
- Use voice commands when available
- Pull over to handle complex tasks

## Cognitive Distraction

Even hands-free devices cause mental distraction:
- Reaction time slows down
- Situational awareness decreases
- **Limit** all non-driving activities

## Company Policy

Our zero-tolerance policy prohibits:
- Texting while driving
- Using handheld devices
- Eating while vehicle is in motion
