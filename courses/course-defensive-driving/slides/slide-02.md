# The 3-Second Rule

## What is the 3-Second Rule?

The 3-second rule helps you maintain a safe following distance from the vehicle ahead.

## How to Apply It

1. **Pick a fixed point ahead** (sign, tree, marking)
2. **When the vehicle ahead passes it, start counting** ("one thousand one, one thousand two, one thousand three")
3. **You should reach that point after 3+ seconds**

## When to Increase Distance

| Condition | Recommended Distance |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Normal driving | 3 seconds |
| Wet roads | 4 seconds |
| Heavy rain | 5 seconds |
| Fog/Ice | 6+ seconds |
| Following large vehicles | 4-5 seconds |

## Why It Works

At 60 km/h, you travel approximately 17 meters per second. A 3-second gap gives you:
- Time to perceive danger
- Time to react and brake
- Space to stop safely

## Common Mistakes

- Following too closely in traffic
- Not adjusting for weather conditions
- Tailgating when in a hurry
